France has more campsites than almost anywhere on Earth, and the range is
extraordinary: wild Cévennes valleys, surf beaches, vineyard farms, and
high-Alpine meadows. These ten regions and sites stand out.

## 1. Gorges du Verdon, Provence

Pitches above turquoise water in Europe's grandest canyon — kayaking below,
cicadas above.

## 2. Île de Ré, Atlantic coast

Pine-backed dune campsites linked by cycle paths to oyster villages and white
beaches.

## 3. Chamonix valley, Haute-Savoie

Camp with Mont Blanc filling the window — a classic Alpine base for walkers and
climbers.

## 4. Dordogne / Vézère valley

Riverside camping beneath clifftop châteaux and prehistoric caves.

## 5. Cap Fréhel & the Côtes-d'Armor, Brittany

Wild, weather-swept Atlantic headland camping with dramatic coast paths.

## 6. Lac d'Annecy, Savoie

Lakeshore sites beside Europe's cleanest big lake, ringed by mountains.

## 7. The Cévennes, Occitanie

Quiet, dark-sky camping in a national park made for slow walking.

## 8. Camargue, Rhône delta

Flat, wild wetland camping among flamingos, white horses, and salt light.

## 9. Corsica — Restonica & the west coast

Mountain-river and beach camping on the most dramatic Mediterranean island.

## 10. Vosges lakes, Grand Est

Forested lake camping, gentle and uncrowded, ideal for a first French trip.

## How French camping works

France grades campsites by stars and splits broadly into big holiday parks
(pools, restaurants, mobile homes) and quieter *campings municipaux* (cheap,
simple, village-run) plus farm camping (*Accueil Paysan*, France Passion for
vans). Wild camping is restricted, especially in national parks and on the
coast — use sites, and ask landowners elsewhere.

## When to go

July–August is glorious but booked out and busy on the coast; June and
September give warm weather, space, and lower prices. The Alps are a
summer game; the Mediterranean shoulder seasons are the connoisseur's choice.

## Booking and etiquette

Popular coastal and gorge sites fill months ahead for high summer — reserve
early. Respect quiet hours, pack out everything, keep fires only where
explicitly allowed (a real wildfire risk in the south), and leave pitches
cleaner than you found them.
