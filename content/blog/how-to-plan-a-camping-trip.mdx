A good camping trip is mostly decided before you leave the driveway. Work
through these steps in order and the trip itself becomes the easy part.

## Step 1: Pick the trip type

Be honest about the trip: serviced car-camping (facilities, comfort), touring
(a new site each night), or wild/backcountry (self-sufficient). They need
different gear and planning. Most great trips — and all good first ones — are
serviced car-camping.

## Step 2: Choose and book the site

Use the [map](/) to find campsites in your target area, then check each for
facilities, access, and whether it takes bookings. Popular lake, coast, and
national-park sites fill months ahead for summer — reserve early and confirm
opening dates (many are seasonal).

## Step 3: Plan the route and timing

Keep driving days short, especially with a tent to pitch. Aim to arrive with
at least two hours of daylight. For touring trips, plan realistic hops and
identify a fallback site for each night.

## Step 4: Build the gear list around four needs

Shelter (tent tested at home, pegs, mallet, groundsheet), sleep (mat with a
warm rating, bag, pillow), warmth (layers, hat — nights are colder than you
think), and cooking (stove, fuel, pan, lighter, water containers). Write it as
a checklist and tick it physically.

## Step 5: Plan food deliberately

Decide every meal before you go. Simple, one-pot dinners, no-cook lunches, and
a reliable breakfast. Pre-prep and pack a cool box. Carry more water than you
expect if the site's supply is uncertain.

## Step 6: Read the weather — and respect it

Check the forecast in the days before and pack for the actual conditions, not
the hopeful ones. Cold and wet are the trip-killers: a warm bag, a dry change
of clothes, and a tarp solve most of it. Wind is the underrated hazard — pitch
sheltered and peg out fully.

## Step 7: Have a bail-out plan

Know how to leave: nearest town, a backup site, where you would go if the
weather turns dangerous. A clear exit is what makes camping relaxing rather
than a commitment you have to endure.

## Step 8: Camp responsibly

Leave no trace: pack out all rubbish, use facilities provided, fires only where
permitted and fully extinguished, keep quiet hours, and protect water sources.
The next campers — and the place itself — depend on it.

## Put it together

Type, then booked site, then route, then a checklist of the four needs, then
food, then weather, then a bail-out. Open the [map](/), pick the site, and the
rest of a great camping trip falls into place.
