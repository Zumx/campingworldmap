Some campsites are destinations in their own right — places where the pitch is
the point and the view is the trip. These are the ones campers cross the world
for.

## Torres del Paine, Chile

Camps strung along the "W" and "O" circuits beneath sheer granite towers — the
benchmark trekking-camp experience on Earth.

## Wadi Rum, Jordan

Bedouin desert camps among red sandstone monoliths under some of the darkest,
starriest skies anywhere.

## Serengeti, Tanzania

Mobile safari camps that move with the great migration — falling asleep to
lions is the experience.

## Lofoten Islands, Norway

Beach and fjord pitches under midnight sun and, in winter, the aurora — Arctic
camping at its most cinematic.

## Yosemite Valley, USA

Camping beneath El Capitan and Half Dome in the cathedral of American
camping.

## Sahara — Erg Chebbi, Morocco

Dune camps reached by camel, silent but for sand — the classic desert night.

## Banff & the Canadian Rockies

Glacier-fed lake camping under turquoise water and ice — Lake Louise country.

## Milford / Routeburn, New Zealand

Hut-and-camp pitches in fjordland rainforest and alpine pass scenery.

## Dolomites, Italy

Mountain-meadow camping beneath pale vertical limestone, an alpine classic.

## Namib-Naukluft, Namibia

Camps beside the world's tallest dunes, with night skies that redefine "dark."

## What makes a campsite impressive

Sometimes it is the setting (Patagonia, the Dolomites), sometimes the silence
and the stars (the Sahara, Namibia), and sometimes the wildlife sleeping nearby
(the Serengeti). The greatest campsites trade comfort for an experience no
hotel can buy: being inside the landscape, not looking at it.

## Visiting the icons responsibly

These places are fragile and often protected. Many require permits, booked
pitches, or licensed operators (Torres del Paine, Serengeti, parts of Yosemite
and New Zealand's Great Walks) — book months ahead. Leave absolutely no trace,
keep wildlife wild (store food correctly), stick to designated pitches, and
use operators that protect the land they profit from.
