Sweden treats camping as a basic right, not a niche hobby. Allemansrätten —
the right of public access — lets you pitch a tent almost anywhere for a
night, and the country backs that up with a network of well-run campgrounds
in the most beautiful places it has. These ten are the ones worth planning
around.

## 1. Kebnekaise Fjällstation, Lappland

The gateway camp for Sweden's highest mountain, in a glacial valley above
Nikkaluokta. The STF station is the base; pitch a tent on the meadows below
or push deeper into the Kungsleden.

## 2. Abisko, Lappland

A campground inside Abisko National Park, on the shore of Torneträsk and at
the start of the classic Kungsleden trail. In winter, one of the most
reliable aurora viewing spots on Earth.

## 3. Stora Sjöfallet, Laponia

The southern entry to Sarek, in the UNESCO Laponia world heritage area.
Serious wilderness immediately behind the site — Sarek itself has no
facilities at all.

## 4. Grövelsjön, Dalarna

A mountain station and camping area on the Norwegian border, in low fells
covered in cloudberry and reindeer. Easier than the far north, with the same
silence.

## 5. Fulufjället, Dalarna

The campsite at the foot of Sweden's highest waterfall, Njupeskär, and inside
one of the country's quieter national parks. A short drive from Idre but a
world away from it.

## 6. Tiveden National Park, Västra Götaland

A primeval boulder forest between lakes Vättern and Vänern, with a campsite
at the park edge and dispersed pitching inside. Small, dense, eerie — the
best low-altitude wilderness in southern Sweden.

## 7. Stockholm Archipelago — Grinda and Finnhamn

STF-run island sites a couple of hours by ferry from central Stockholm.
Pitch on granite slabs, swim in the Baltic, watch the ferries pass at
sunset. The accessible version of Swedish wild camping.

## 8. Gotland — Norsholmen and Fårö coast

Sites along the limestone coast of Fårö and northern Gotland, beside
raukar — wind-sculpted sea stacks. Dry, sunny, and unlike anywhere else in
the country.

## 9. Söderåsen National Park, Skåne

A campsite at Skäralid on the edge of a beech-forested ravine, the deepest
in northern Europe. The southern Swedish answer to the rest of this list —
greener, gentler, and accessible year-round.

## 10. Koster Islands, Bohuslän

Sweden's only marine national park, with sites on North and South Koster
reached by ferry from Strömstad. Bicycles only, clean water, smooth pink
granite — the west-coast counterpart to the Stockholm archipelago.

## How allemansrätten actually works

You can pitch one or two tents on uncultivated land for one night, as long
as you are out of sight of houses and well clear of crops, gardens, and
livestock. You may not light open fires in dry conditions or on bare rock
(rock cracks from heat), and the rule is "do not disturb, do not destroy."
The right exists because Swedes use it responsibly; behave the same way.

## Season and booking

The viable camping season in the far north is roughly mid-June to mid-September —
outside that, you need real winter skills. STF (Svenska Turistföreningen)
runs the mountain stations and many campgrounds; book pitches and huts on
their site weeks ahead for July and August. Mosquitoes peak in July in
Lappland; bring serious repellent or come in late August.
