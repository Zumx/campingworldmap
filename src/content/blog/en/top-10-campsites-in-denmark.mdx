Denmark has one of the highest rates of camping participation per capita in Europe. The Naturlejrpladser (nature camping areas) system and the commercial campsite network together provide coverage across the country's modest geography — no point in Denmark is more than 52 km from the coast. The Danish camping standard (Campingrådet classification, 1-5 stars) is well-maintained and provides reliable guidance for facilities quality. July is peak season and sites fill rapidly; June and August are significantly more relaxed. The Danish tradition of cyclist-friendly camping — many sites have cycling trails connecting them — makes a touring itinerary viable without a vehicle.

## 1. Skagen Nordstrand Camping, Skagen, North Jutland

At the northernmost tip of Jutland, where the Skagerrak and Kattegat meet, Skagen is defined by its extraordinary northern light — the quality that attracted the Skagen Painters in the nineteenth century. The campsite is close to Grenen, the sand spit at the tip of Denmark. The light at sunset over the meeting of two seas is genuinely exceptional. Electric pitches, good sanitary facilities. Skagen town's painted yellow houses and the buried Tilsandede Kirke (the Sand-Buried Church) are within cycling distance.

## 2. Råbjerg Mile Camping, Hirtshals, North Jutland

Adjacent to Råbjerg Mile — Europe's largest migrating sand dune, covering roughly 1 square kilometre and moving 15-18 metres per year across the Jutland landscape — this is one of Denmark's most unusual campsite settings. The dune itself is a natural spectacle; the campsite facilities are solid, and the North Sea coastline is accessible within a few kilometres.

## 3. Silkeborg Sø Camping, Silkeborg, Central Jutland

On the Silkeborg Lakes — a system of glacier-carved lakes in the Jutland highlands — this campsite provides kayaking and swimming access to some of Denmark's cleanest inland water. Silkeborg is also the location of the Tollund Man, one of the best-preserved bog bodies in the world, on display at the town museum. The lakeside cycling infrastructure here is exceptional.

## 4. Hesselhus Naturcamping, Odsherred, Zealand

In the Odsherred peninsula — a UNESCO-designated Geopark for its glacial landscape — Hesselhus is a smaller, nature-focused site on the shore of the Isefjord. The Odsherred landscape of hummocky moraine terrain, meadows, and fjord inlets is representative of the Danish lakeland character at its most accessible from Copenhagen (90 minutes by road).

## 5. Dueodde Feriecenter, Bornholm

At the white sand beach of Dueodde on the southern tip of Bornholm — Denmark's Baltic island — this campsite is the best base for the island's remarkable beaches. Bornholm's geology, a granite-and-sandstone platform that produces white sand beaches unlike anywhere else in Denmark, combined with the island's excellent smoked herring (røget sild) and arts tradition, makes it a distinct camping destination. Ferry access from Ystad (Sweden) or Køge.

## 6. Mons Klint Camping, Møn

Near the Møns Klint chalk cliffs — the most dramatic coastal geology in Denmark, with white cliffs rising 120 metres from the Baltic — this campsite provides direct access to the cliff-top walking paths and fossil hunting on the beach below. Møn is also one of the best locations for dark sky astronomy in Denmark, designated a Dark Sky Park. Electric pitches, good facilities.

## 7. Sønderho Camping, Fanø Island

On Fanø — a North Sea barrier island reachable by ferry from Esbjerg — the camping at Sønderho village is in the dune landscape behind the beach. Fanø's beaches are among the longest in Denmark, the dune system is well-preserved, and the western-facing coast produces reliable North Sea wind conditions for kitesurfing. The village of Sønderho, with its thatched farmhouses, is a national architectural heritage area.

## 8. Esrum Sø Camping, Fredensborg, North Zealand

On Esrum Lake — Denmark's second-largest lake — in the forested north Zealand landscape 40 km from Copenhagen. The campsite combines good lake swimming with proximity to Fredensborg Palace (the Danish royal family's spring and autumn residence) and the beech forests of Gribskov. Cycling tracks link the site to both the lake shore and the forest interior.

## 9. Camping Møldrup, Viborg, Central Jutland

Near Viborg — the historic ecclesiastical centre of Jutland — on the edge of the Hald Ege lake system. The landscape here is heath and ancient forest, and the lake provides swimming and kayaking. Viborg Cathedral and the Hald Slot castle ruins are day-trip targets from the site.

## 10. Klitgården Naturcamping, Thyborøn, West Jutland

On the narrow Thyborøn strip between the North Sea and the Limfjord, this nature campsite provides a genuine North Sea coastal experience without resort infrastructure. The strip is geologically dynamic — shifting with North Sea storms and famous for the power of its waves. Electric pitches available; the site is compact and well-positioned for cycling the Limfjord route.

## Cycling and walking from campsite to campsite

Denmark's national cycling routes (14 numbered routes) link campsite to campsite across the country. The Hærvej route down the Jutland spine, the coastal routes around Bornholm, and the North Jutland coastal circuit are all well-maintained with regular campsite provision. The Campingrådet app provides current availability and booking for the majority of Danish sites.
