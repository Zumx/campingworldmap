Tanzania holds the largest intact wildlife migration on earth, the highest free-standing mountain in the world, the deepest lake in Africa, and the Zanzibar archipelago off its Indian Ocean coast. Camping ranges from the technical high camps on Kilimanjaro — where altitude sickness is a genuine medical consideration — to the unfenced public campsites of the Serengeti, where lions walk through at night. The Tanzania National Parks (TANAPA) system manages most public camping; the Ngorongoro Conservation Area Authority runs the crater sites separately. All camping inside national parks requires a fee in addition to the park entry fees.

## 1. Mawenzi Tarn Huts Camp, Kilimanjaro

At 4,330 metres on the Rongai or northern circuit routes on Kilimanjaro, the Mawenzi Tarn camp sits in a glacially carved cirque beneath the Mawenzi tower — the second summit of Kilimanjaro, technically more demanding than Uhuru Peak but rarely climbed. The tarn camp is the standard acclimatisation stop on the northern routes; the walk from Kibo Hut to Uhuru Peak (5,895 m) begins at midnight from the camp above. The high camp system on all Kilimanjaro routes is mandatory and managed through TANAPA-registered operators.

## 2. Jakobsen Camping Area, Kilimanjaro

In the lower forest zone of the Kilimanjaro National Park, Jakobsen is one of the lower camps on the Lemosho or Machame routes — the transition zone between the Kilimanjaro cultivation belt and the cloud forest. The forest here holds colobus monkeys, forest elephants (at lower frequency), and extraordinary epiphytic plant assemblages including giant tree heathers hung with old man's beard lichen.

## 3. Lake Natron Camp Site, Arusha Region

At the base of Ol Doinyo Lengai — the world's only active carbonatite volcano — on the shore of Lake Natron in the Rift Valley, this remote campsite is one of Tanzania's most unusual camping experiences. Lake Natron is hypersaline and caustic; the flamingo breeding colony here is the largest in Africa, with up to 2.5 million lesser flamingos nesting on the lake's crust. The Rift Valley walls rise steeply to the east and west.

## 4. Ndarakwai Camping, West Kilimanjaro

In the private Ndarakwai Ranch at the western foot of Kilimanjaro, this campsite provides walking safaris and game drives in an area that functions as a wildlife corridor between Kilimanjaro and the Amboseli ecosystem in Kenya. Elephant, buffalo, giraffe, and good bird diversity are camp regulars. The Kilimanjaro summit (snow-capped in the dry season) is visible directly from camp.

## 5. Sekimba Camp, Mahale Mountains

In the Mahale Mountains National Park on the shore of Lake Tanganyika — accessible only by boat from Kigoma (a 10-hour journey) or by light aircraft — Sekimba camp is one of Africa's most remote camping experiences. Mahale has the largest habituated chimpanzee population in Africa; the 1,000-individual M-group community has been studied continuously since 1965. Lake Tanganyika is 673 metres deep — the deepest lake in Africa — with extraordinary underwater visibility and endemic cichlid fish.

## 6. Empakaai Special Campsite, Ngorongoro

On the rim of Empakaai Crater — a smaller, lesser-known volcanic caldera within the Ngorongoro Conservation Area whose crater floor is partially filled with a flamingo-rich soda lake — this exclusive campsite requires booking through the NCAA and provides an alternative to the more crowded Ngorongoro Crater itself. The walk into Empakaai Crater and around the lake is one of the best crater walks in the region.

## 7. Magara Community Camp Site, Lake Victoria, Kagera

On the western shore of Lake Victoria near the Kagera region, this community camping site is representative of the low-impact camping infrastructure being developed on Tanzania's mainland lake shore. Lake Victoria — Africa's largest lake and the world's second-largest freshwater lake by area — has its own endemic cichlid biodiversity, most of which was devastated by the introduced Nile Perch in the 1980s. Some endemic species are recovering.

## 8. Panorama Camping, Arusha

Near the city of Arusha — Tanzania's northern circuit hub and staging point for Serengeti, Ngorongoro, and Kilimanjaro — Panorama Camping is the most used base camp for camping travellers organising northern circuit safaris. The camp has full facilities and tour operator affiliates on site. Mount Meru (4,562 m), Tanzania's second-highest peak, is accessible from Arusha as a three-day guided trekking option.

## 9. Masai Camp, Northern Serengeti

Inside the Serengeti National Park on the northern circuit, the Masai public campsite provides access to the wildebeest river crossing area. The Mara river crossings occur July through October as the migration moves north into Kenya and back; a northern Serengeti camp puts you at the crossing point without the transfer from the central Seronera area. Public sites are unfenced; all food must be secured in vehicles.

## 10. Waterfall Camp Site, Southern Highlands

In the Kipengere Range of the Southern Highlands near Mbeya, this campsite is associated with a waterfall on the escarpment above the Great Rift Valley. The Southern Highlands receive significantly more rainfall than the northern circuit area and support a completely different vegetation and bird community. The Udzungwa Mountains National Park, accessible from the highway, has endemic bird and primate species found nowhere else.

## Kilimanjaro camping regulations

All Kilimanjaro ascending parties must be organised through a licensed TANAPA operator. Independent camping on the mountain is prohibited. The operator provides guides, porters, cooking staff, and camping equipment. The operator is responsible for altitude safety monitoring; a pulse oximeter and awareness of AMS symptoms are required knowledge before departure.
