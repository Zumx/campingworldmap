The United States National Park System manages over 400 sites covering 34 million hectares, with a campsite network that includes everything from drive-in sites with electric hookups to multi-day backcountry sites reachable only by river. The reservation system at recreation.gov controls access to the most popular sites; some open months in advance and fill within seconds of the reservation window. The parks range from the iconic western destinations — Yosemite, Grand Canyon, Yellowstone — to the less-visited backcountry of the Olympic Peninsula, Everglades, and Great Smoky Mountains. State park systems add thousands more sites, many with better availability than the federal parks.

## 1. North Pines Campground, Yosemite Valley, California

In the valley floor at the east end of Yosemite Valley, North Pines is positioned between the Merced River and the valley wall with direct views of Half Dome and Clouds Rest from camp. The iconic Yosemite landmarks — El Capitan, Half Dome, Bridalveil Fall — are within a few kilometres. Reservations are mandatory and open five months in advance; they sell out within minutes. Bear-proof food storage containers are mandatory in the valley.

## 2. Cape Royal Campground, North Rim, Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona

The North Rim of the Grand Canyon — less visited and 300 metres higher than the South Rim — is accessible for only five months per year (mid-May through mid-October) before snow closes the road. The North Rim campground at 2,500 metres elevation provides a significantly cooler base than the South Rim and direct access to the Angel's Gate and Walhalla Plateau overlooks. The Trans-Canyon Trail to the South Rim begins here; it requires a permit and two to three days.

## 3. Lostman's Five Campsite, Everglades National Park, Florida

In the Ten Thousand Islands section of Everglades National Park, accessible only by kayak or canoe from Everglades City. The Wilderness Waterway — a marked 160 km canoe route through the mangrove estuary — links a series of chickee platforms (raised tent platforms over the water) and ground sites. Lostman's Five is a ground site on a shell mound island with good camping for self-sufficient kayakers. Alligators, American crocodiles, roseate spoonbills, and manatee are regular wildlife.

## 4. Ozette Triangle Campsites, Olympic National Park, Washington

On the Olympic Peninsula's wilderness coast — one of the last sections of wild Pacific coast in the contiguous United States — the campsites at Sand Point and Cape Alava (the westernmost point reachable by trail in the lower 48) are accessed by a 5-7 km boardwalk trail through temperate rainforest. The beach itself — driftwood logs, sea stacks, tidal pools — is unlike most accessible US coastline. Sea otters, harbour seals, and grey whales offshore.

## 5. High Bluff Campground, Torrey Pines State Natural Reserve, California

At the cliff top above the Torrey Pine groves on the San Diego County coast, High Bluff is one of the most scenically positioned state park campsites in California. Torrey pines (Pinus torreyana) grow naturally only here and on Santa Rosa Island — the rarest pine in North America. The campsite has direct trail access to the grove and to the beach below the cliffs. Reservations through ReserveCalifornia.

## 6. Chisos Basin Campground, Big Bend National Park, Texas

In the mountain bowl of the Chisos Range at 1,600 metres altitude — a sky island of cooler microclimate within the Chihuahuan Desert — the Chisos Basin camp is the most scenic in Big Bend. The window view (a V-shaped notch in the basin rim) frames the desert sunset; the Emory Peak trail to the highest point in the park begins adjacent to the site. Javelinas are common camp visitors; mountain lions are present but rarely seen.

## 7. Wonderland Trail Campsites, Mount Rainier National Park, Washington

The 150 km Wonderland Trail circuits Mount Rainier — the most heavily glaciated peak in the lower 48 states. The 18 designated wilderness campsites around the mountain require a permit ($20 per night, through recreation.gov). The trail crosses seven distinct life zones from old-growth forest to glacier-margin terrain. The mountain's circumference takes 10-14 days at standard pace.

## 8. Dogwood Campsite, Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Tennessee/North Carolina

In the most visited national park in the United States (by total annual visitors), the backcountry campsites along the Appalachian Trail corridor in the Smokies are significantly less crowded than the frontcountry sites. The Smokies hold the most biologically diverse temperate forest in North America — over 100 tree species and 1,500 flowering plant species. The park straddles the Tennessee-North Carolina border; the AT follows the ridge throughout.

## 9. River Camp, Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness, Minnesota

The BWCAW — one million acres of Boundary Waters lakes and forest in northern Minnesota — has an extensive canoe camping permit system that limits daily entry by route. The portage-connected lakes support a multi-day canoe camping tradition that is unique in the lower 48. Loon calling, moose, timber wolf, and lake trout fishing define the experience. Sites are primitive — fire grate, latrine, no other infrastructure.

## 10. South Bay RV Park and Campground, Chula Vista, California

At the southern end of San Diego Bay near the Tijuana border, this campsite provides access to the San Diego Bay National Wildlife Refuge — one of the most productive shorebird and wading bird habitats on the Pacific coast. American avocet, black-necked stilt, and Ridgway's rail (an endangered species restricted to coastal southern California) are present. The campsite's urban location contrasts with good wildlife access.

## Recreation.gov and reservation strategy

The most popular NPS campgrounds open reservations 6 months in advance at midnight Eastern time. High-demand sites (Yosemite Valley, Grand Canyon South Rim, Cape Hatteras) sell out within seconds using automated booking tools. Check cancellation availability regularly — cancellations appear throughout the season. Permit-only backcountry sites operate separate lotteries with deadlines months ahead.
