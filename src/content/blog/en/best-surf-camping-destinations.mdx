Surf camping — living in a tent or van within walking distance of the water, structuring the day entirely around tides, swell direction, and light — is one of the purest forms of travel surfing. It removes the hotel-to-beach commute, allows you to observe the waves for multiple days and understand how a spot works across different conditions, and puts you in the water at first light when crowds are absent and the surface is typically glassy before the onshore wind develops. The greatest surf camping destinations tend to combine consistent, quality waves with camping access close enough to the shore that you can see the break from your tent.

Surf camping works equally well for van lifers who spend months following swells along a coastline and for tent campers who fly to a destination and rely on local camping infrastructure. The two models require different preparation — a van offers flexibility and storage for boards, wetsuits, and gear that tent camping from a flight cannot — but both access the same essential experience: waking up, checking the surf, and deciding the day from there. What follows is a global selection of destinations where the combination of waves and camping access is exceptional.

## Jeffrey's Bay, Eastern Cape, South Africa

Supertubes at Jeffrey's Bay is widely considered one of the ten best right-hand point breaks in the world. The wave peels for several hundred metres across multiple connected sections — Boneyards, Supertubes, Impossibles, Point — and when a solid south swell lines up with south wind, it produces the long, fast, barrel-section rides that have made J-Bay famous in surfing media. Several backpacker lodges and campgrounds in the town provide basic accommodation within walking distance of the break. The Billabong Pro, a WSL Championship Tour event, runs at Supertubes in July. Water temperature requires a 3/2 wetsuit for most visitors; the Eastern Cape coast is cold even in summer.

## The Basque Country, Spain and France

The Basque coastline straddles the French-Spanish border around Biarritz and San Sebastian and contains a concentration of quality surf breaks — beach breaks, point breaks, and reef breaks — within a compact geographic area. Hossegor in France is particularly known for its heavy, hollow beach breaks on powerful Atlantic swells. Camping is well established throughout the region, with several well-run campsites between Biarritz and Hossegor offering sites within cycling distance of the breaks. The surf season runs from September through April when Atlantic low-pressure systems generate consistent swell; summer waves exist but are smaller and the crowds more intense.

## Baja California, Mexico

Baja California's Pacific coast below Ensenada offers a long sequence of point breaks, reef breaks, and beach breaks along a sparsely populated peninsula accessible by the Transpeninsular Highway. The stretch from San Miguel through Punta Conejo and down to Scorpion Bay (San Juanico) in Baja California Sur has multiple quality spots with adjacent camping directly on the beach. Scorpion Bay is a legendary long right-hand point break — under the right swell, the wave connects for more than half a kilometre — with a small palapa campground above the beach. The entire Baja Peninsula is suited to van-based surf camping; the highway is paved throughout and fuel is available at reasonable intervals.

## Raglan, New Zealand

Raglan on New Zealand's Waikato west coast is the archetypal New Zealand surf town — a small, relaxed community built around a series of point breaks that produce some of the longest left-handers in the Southern Hemisphere. Manu Bay and Whale Bay are the main breaks, and both are accessible by short walk from the town. A Department of Conservation campground at Wainui Beach and several holiday parks near Raglan provide tent and van camping within the surf zone. The Raglan region experiences consistent swell from the Tasman Sea throughout the year, with winter producing the most powerful surf.

## Mundaka, Basque Country, Spain

Mundaka in the Spanish Basque Country is home to one of the great left-hand rivermouth barrels in European surfing. The wave forms where the Urdaibai estuary meets the sea, creating a sand-bottom barrel that can run for 400 metres under the right conditions. The wave is extremely sensitive to sand movement in the estuary and has at times virtually disappeared for extended periods; at its best it is world-class. A campsite in Mundaka town provides the closest available camping, and the Basque coast's broader surf range means nearby alternatives are available when Mundaka is flat.

## The Mentawai Islands, Indonesia

The Mentawai Islands off the west coast of Sumatra in Indonesia are among the most consistently excellent surf zones on the planet, with multiple world-class breaks spread across Siberut, Sipura, and the Pagai islands. Camping in the Mentawais means either a surf charter boat (the most common arrangement for visiting surfers, sleeping on a live-aboard vessel anchored near breaks) or staying at one of the surf camps established near key spots. Land-based camping is less common than the boat charter model due to the islands' geography and limited land access to reef breaks. The surf season is April through October.

## Hossegor, France

Hossegor's beach breaks on the Landes coast south of Bordeaux produce fast, hollow waves that have made the town the surf capital of France and one of the premier surf destinations in Europe. The Quiksilver Pro and Roxy Pro (WSL Championship Tour events) run at Hossegor in September when autumn swells begin arriving. Multiple campgrounds in the Landes pine forest a short cycle from the beach provide well-priced camping in an established surf community. The forest setting — vast, flat, shaded by maritime pines — gives Hossegor a distinctive character different from more exposed Atlantic surf towns.

## Pacific Coast Highway, California, USA

California's PCH offers one of the great surf road trips, with quality breaks — Rincon at the Santa Barbara-Ventura county line, Malibu, El Porto, Trestles, Swami's, Ocean Beach — strung along 1,000 kilometres of coastline. Camping is available at several state beaches directly above the water: Leo Carrillo State Park, San Onofre State Beach (home of the famous Trestles surf area), and Emma Wood State Beach near Ventura. These state beach campgrounds book out months in advance in summer. Surf camping on the PCH means adjusting daily plans around swell forecasts from services like Surfline and Magic Seaweed.

## Planning a Surf Camping Trip

Good surf camping requires checking forecasts carefully before departure. Surf forecasting services provide swell height, period, and direction data that, combined with local knowledge about how specific breaks perform in specific conditions, allows you to target your travel for productive surf windows. [Open the map](/map) to find campgrounds near surf coasts around the world and plan the driving distances and logistics between spots. A wetsuit appropriate for the water temperature, a board bag that functions as a travel case, and a basic surf wax kit are the essentials of a self-sufficient surf camp kit. Everything else — the right swell, the right wind, the uncrowded morning session — depends on timing and luck.
