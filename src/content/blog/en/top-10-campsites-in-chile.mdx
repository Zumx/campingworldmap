Chile is the most geographically extreme country in the world by latitude — 4,300 kilometres from the Atacama Desert in the north to Cape Horn in the south. Its camping landscape is correspondingly varied: high-altitude desert at 4,500 metres in the Atacama, the temperate rainforest of Chiloé and the Carretera Austral, the Patagonian steppe of the far south, and the volcanic lake district of the Araucanía. The CONAF national park system manages most wilderness camping, and Torres del Paine in the south is one of the world's most visited camping circuits.

## 1. Camping Grey, Torres del Paine National Park

On the western arm of the Torres del Paine W circuit, Camping Grey sits beside the Grey Glacier with iceberg-dotted lake views. The campsite is CONAF-managed with basic facilities and a refugio. It is accessible on foot from Paine Grande or by catamaran from Pudeto. Wind is extreme at this location — tent pegging is essential, and four-season tents are recommended even in summer. The glacier face has retreated significantly over the past two decades.

## 2. Campamento Paine Grande, Torres del Paine

The central hub of the W circuit, Paine Grande is the most-trafficked campsite in Patagonian Chile. It sits at the junction of the W's three arms with refugio accommodation and a basic services complex. Reservations through the official CONAF/Vertice/Mountain Lodge booking system are mandatory; the system opens approximately one year in advance for peak season. The site's exposure to Patagonian wind is significant — a fully staked tent is not optional here.

## 3. Campamento Chileno, Torres del Paine

On the eastern arm of the W, at the base of the canyon approach to the Torres themselves. Chileno is the overnight camp for the dawn ascent to the mirador — a 45-minute scramble above the campsite that gives the classic Torres del Paine view of the three towers above the lake. The site is tent-only; no refugio accommodation. Arrive early to secure a flat pitch.

## 4. Camping El Volcán, Cajón del Maipo, Metropolitan Region

Less than 90 km from Santiago, Camping El Volcán sits in the Cajón del Maipo gorge at 1,850 metres altitude with views to the Volcán San José and the surrounding Andean peaks. The site is a practical weekend escape for Santiago residents — accessible by car with good facilities, electric pitches, and excellent day hiking and rock climbing access. Popular and busy on weekends; mid-week visits are significantly quieter.

## 5. Parque Nacional Chiloé Campsite, Chiloé Island

On the Pacific-facing coast of Chiloé — Chile's second-largest island — the national park holds one of the last intact stands of temperate Valdivian rainforest on the island. The campsite at Cole Cole requires a 7 km walk along beach and forest from the road. Black-necked swans and sea otters are commonly observed; the beach is extraordinary. Boat taxis operate from Cucao village. The Chiloé fog and rain are consistent; waterproofing everything is mandatory.

## 6. Camping Río Gonzalo, Carretera Austral, Aysén

On the Carretera Austral — the gravel road built through the otherwise trackless Chilean patagonia — Camping Río Gonzalo is adjacent to a significant river with fly fishing. The Carretera Austral camping circuit is distinct from the Torres del Paine circuit; it attracts fewer international visitors and retains a more genuinely exploratory character. The road itself is an adventure by vehicle.

## 7. Camping Los Cuernos, Torres del Paine

On the northern shore of Lago Nordenskjöld in the middle section of the W circuit, with direct views of the Cuernos del Paine — the horned granite peaks that give the range its dramatic profile. The camp has refugio facilities alongside camping pitches. Wind off the lake is significant; the lake itself provides cold-water swimming for the hardy.

## 8. Camping Catrué, Conguillio National Park, Araucanía

In the shadow of the active Volcán Llaima (3,125 m) in the Araucanía region's Conguillio National Park. The park contains the largest concentration of monkey puzzle (araucaria) trees in the world — ancient living fossils up to 1,000 years old. The campsite has electric pitches and sanitary facilities. Llaima's eruptive history requires attention; the CONAF emergency protocol should be read at the ranger station.

## 9. Camping Renahue, Los Lagos

In the Los Lagos region on the border of the temperate rainforest zone, Renahue provides river camping in a landscape of dense lenga beech and quilineja bamboo. Less developed and quieter than the main circuit sites, it suits self-sufficient campers exploring the lake district independently.

## 10. Termas San Sebastián Camp, Araucanía

Adjacent to natural hot spring pools on a river in the Andean foothills east of Temuco, this site is popular with Chilean weekenders and combines hot-spring bathing with forest river camping. The thermal pools range from 38 to 45 degrees Celsius. Facilities are basic; the draw is the thermal access.

## Booking and season

Torres del Paine camping must be reserved through the centralised CONAF system well in advance for November through March. The Carretera Austral sites are generally first-come-first-served. October and November are less crowded than December through February; April is still viable weather-wise in northern Patagonia.
