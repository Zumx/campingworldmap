Sweden's allemansrätten — the right of public access — permits camping on any land not immediately adjacent to a private dwelling for up to one or two nights without the landowner's permission. In a country of 450,000 lakes, 63% forest cover, and dramatic terrain from the Arctic fells of Lapland to the granite coast of Bohuslän, this right produces one of the world's finest wild camping environments. The organised campsite network (SCR, Swedish Camping) is well-developed and graded 1-5 stars, complementing the wild camping tradition rather than replacing it.

## 1. Abisko Turiststation, Abisko National Park, Lapland

At the northern end of the Kungsleden — the 440 km "King's Trail" from Abisko to Hemavan — the Abisko tourist station provides the official start point and camping infrastructure for Sweden's most famous long-distance hiking route. The campsite has electric pitches and facilities; the wild camping along the Kungsleden itself is permitted throughout in the designated nature reserves. The Northern Lights season runs September through March; the midnight sun from June through July.

## 2. Nikkaluokta Camp, Gällivare, Lapland

At the trailhead for the Kebnekaise massif approach, Nikkaluokta provides camping for those beginning the 18 km walk to the Kebnekaise Mountain Station — the base for Sweden's highest summit (Kebnekaise South, 2,096 m). The surrounding Saami territory is active reindeer country; the herds move through the valley seasonally. The Sámi village and the traditional joik musical tradition are part of the cultural context.

## 3. STF Kungsleden Camp, Sarek National Park approach

Sarek is the most demanding national park in Scandinavia — no marked trails, no facilities, no bridges on many river crossings, and a reputation for sudden weather changes. The camping within Sarek is entirely wild under the allemansrätten; the park has no designated campsites. Entry from the Kvikkjokk or Aktse sides. Arctic fox, wolverine, and Sweden's largest elk population are present. This is for experienced, self-sufficient wilderness campers only.

## 4. First Camp Kolmården, Östergötland

On the Bråviken bay of the Baltic coast near the Kolmården wildlife park in Östergötland, this is one of Sweden's better-equipped commercial campsite experiences — a First Camp branded site with pools, restaurant, and full facilities. The Kolmården zoo, known for Nordic species, is adjacent. The Östergötland coast and the Göta Canal (the nineteenth-century canal crossing Sweden from west to east coast) are accessible from this base.

## 5. Camping Siljan, Dalarna

On Siljan Lake — the largest meteorite impact crater lake in Europe, 75 km in circumference — in the Dalarna region that is considered the most culturally Swedish of Sweden's provinces. The red-painted Falun cottages, Midsommar celebrations, and the traditional folk music of the area are concentrated here. The Vasaloppet cross-country ski race begins annually in Sälen, 150 km northwest; the summer cycling route around Siljan is excellent.

## 6. Camping Fjärås Bräcka, Halland

On the Kungsbacka fjord in Halland — the west coast province south of Gothenburg — this campsite is near the Fjärås Bräcka ridge and the western Swedish lake district. The Halland coast has a distinct character from the more dramatic Bohuslän coast to the north; the Kungsleden Väst (the Western King's Trail, a cycle and walk route) passes through.

## 7. Camping Smögen, Bohuslän

On the Bohuslän coast near the island village of Smögen — the quintessential Swedish west coast holiday destination with red wooden boathouses, shrimp boats, and polished granite islands — this campsite accesses one of Sweden's most distinctively scenic coastal areas. The Bohuslän archipelago stretches 250 km from Gothenburg to the Norwegian border; the coastal camping is among Scandinavia's most popular.

## 8. Camping Björkliden, Abisko, Lapland

Across the Torneträsk lake from Abisko, Björkliden provides an alternative Lapland entry point for the Arctic wilderness. The site has mountain cabins and camping, and the fjell landscape here — open tundra above the birch treeline, ptarmigan, golden plover — is characteristic of Swedish high Lapland. Reindeer frequently walk through the campsite.

## 9. Camping at Söderåsen National Park, Skåne

In the southernmost national park in Sweden, Söderåsen is in a beech forest canyon landscape that is geologically unique in Scandinavia. The park's deep ravines, beech cathedral forests, and ancient oaks are accessible from the campsite outside the park boundary. Red kite — a bird species long extinct in Sweden and recently reintroduced — is established in the Skåne farmland around the park.

## 10. First Camp Gröna Lund, Stockholm area

Within the Stockholm urban area at Kärsön island, this large campsite is one of the most accessible camping options near a Scandinavian capital. The Stockholm archipelago — 30,000 islands and skerries in the Baltic — is accessible by ferry from multiple points. The archipelago wild camping tradition (allemansrätten extends to uninhabited islands) makes a multi-day island kayaking expedition from an urban base a realistic itinerary.

## SCR grading and the camping experience

Swedish Camping (SCR) grades sites 1-5 stars, with the majority of well-maintained sites holding 3-4 stars. Most commercial sites are open June through August; some operate year-round. The annual SCR camping card provides a discount at affiliated sites. Wild camping equipment requirements: a rainfly that handles Nordic conditions, insect protection for June-July Lapland, and the navigation skills to handle unmarked terrain in the national parks.
