Greece's camping infrastructure is built around the coastline and islands, reflecting the country's fundamental identity as a maritime culture. The Greek National Tourism Organisation (GNTO) classifies campsites from A to C, with A-class sites providing full facilities comparable to four-star European standards. The camping season runs effectively from May through October, with July and August extremely busy and priced accordingly. The combination of predictable summer weather, clear Aegean water, and access to classical archaeology makes Greece one of the Mediterranean's most compelling camping destinations. The mainland — particularly the Peloponnese and Epirus — is significantly less crowded than the islands.

## 1. Camping Dionyssos, Kavala, Eastern Macedonia

On the coast near ancient Kavala (the biblical Neapolis where Paul landed on his first European journey), this GNTO A-class site has electric pitches, a restaurant, and direct sea access. The site is a base for the archaeological sites of Philippi and Thassos island — accessible by ferry. The tobacco-growing plain of eastern Macedonia and the Drama wine region are day-trip destinations.

## 2. Camping Akti, Olympiakí Aktí, Pieria

On the Pierian coast south of Thessaloniki, with Mount Olympus (2,918 m) rising directly behind. The campsite has sea access and electric pitches; the Olympus National Park trailhead at Litochoro is 30 km south. The standard Olympus hiking circuit — Litochoro to the Refuge A at 2,100 metres, then to Mytikas summit and return — is one of Greece's most ambitious day hikes.

## 3. Camping Hermione, Peloponnese

On the Peloponnese coast near the town of Ermioni on the Argolid peninsula, this site is representative of the quieter Peloponnese coastal camping that exists at considerable remove from the mass tourism of the islands. The surrounding landscape — ancient olive groves, fishing villages, and the ruins of Mycenae and Epidaurus within day-trip range — is the essential Greek experience for history-focused campers.

## 4. Camping Vrachos, Epirus

At the town of Kokori near the Acheron River in Epirus — in ancient mythology the gateway to the underworld — this camping area is in northern Greece's most dramatic karst landscape. The Acheron springs near Glyki and the Necromanteion site (the ancient Oracle of the Dead) are local destinations. Epirus receives significantly more rainfall than the rest of Greece and stays green well into summer.

## 5. Camping Paleokastritsa, Corfu

On the island of Corfu at the famous Paleokastritsa bay — turquoise water, limestone cliffs, and the Byzantine monastery on the headland — this site is inside the best natural scenery on an island that is otherwise heavily touristic. The Ionian Sea on the western coast of Corfu is calmer and clearer than the east-facing Ionian sites. The site has electric pitches and a restaurant.

## 6. Camping Navagio, Near Zakynthos

The nearby area to the Navagio (Shipwreck) beach — one of the most photographed locations in Greece — accessible only by boat from the north coast of Zakynthos. Camping on the mainland north coast of the island near the boat departure points for Navagio provides the best base. The beach itself, enclosed by vertical white limestone cliffs with the rusting hulk of the Panagiotis freighter, is genuinely spectacular.

## 7. Camping Delphi, Fokida

At the foot of the Parnassos massif below the archaeological site of Delphi — the ancient sanctuary of Apollo and the most important oracle site in the Greek world — this site combines one of the most significant archaeological experiences in Europe with mountain camping. The olive grove approach to Delphi from Amfissa below and the Parnassos ski station above define the elevation range from this base.

## 8. Camping Mithimna, Lesbos

On the island of Lesbos (Mytilene) near the medieval castle town of Mithimna (Molyvos) in the north of the island. Lesbos is the third-largest Greek island and significantly less touristic than Rhodes or Corfu. The island's tradition of ouzo production, its fossilised forest, and the thermal springs at Eftalou are characteristic attractions. The campsite has basic facilities and sea access.

## 9. Camping Finikes, Syros, Cyclades

On Syros — the administrative capital of the Cyclades, a working Greek island rather than a resort island — this small campsite is near the town of Finikas on the island's west coast. Syros has a distinct character, with the neoclassical architecture of Ermoupoli town reflecting its nineteenth-century mercantile prosperity. The island is accessible by ferry from Piraeus and provides a base for island-hopping without the overcrowding of Mykonos or Santorini.

## 10. Camping Thessaloniki, Nea Anchialos

Near Thessaloniki — Greece's second city — this GNTO-listed site provides access to the Byzantine monuments of the city and to Mount Athos (accessible to male visitors by special permit). The Halkidiki peninsula beaches are 40 km east, and the Vergina royal tombs (burial site of Philip II of Macedon) are accessible by day trip.

## Greek camping practicalities

GNTO-licensed campsites are the safest choice in Greece for facilities reliability. Many smaller sites operate informally; the Panhellenic Camping Association lists licensed sites. July and August rates at island sites are considerably higher than May, June, or September. Bring your own shade — midday summer sun at Greek campsites is intense, and many sites have minimal natural shade.
