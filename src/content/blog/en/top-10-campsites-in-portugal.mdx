Portugal's camping network stretches from the Atlantic coast to the Spanish border and from the Minho in the rainy northwest to the Algarve's sun-baked cliffs in the south. The national parks and natural parks system covers 20% of Portuguese territory, and municipal campsite provision is strong throughout — most towns maintain a Parque de Campismo e Caravanismo that is well-priced and reliably maintained. The FCMP (Federação de Campismo e Montanhismo de Portugal) classifies sites from one to five stars. Portugal's camping season is year-round in the south; the north and mountains are best from May through October.

## 1. Parque de Campismo do Vidoeiro, Gerês, Braga

In the Peneda-Gerês National Park — Portugal's only national park — the Vidoeiro campsite is on the Cávado river near the spa town of Gerês. The park holds the largest concentration of oak woodland in Portugal, along with wolf, wild boar, and the Garrano (the small Iberian horse bred here since ancient times). The campsite is shaded, the river swimming excellent, and the Geira Roman road trailhead close by.

## 2. Gerês Greenpark, Gerês, Braga

A private campsite in the Gerês valley combining good facilities — electric pitches, pool, restaurant — with immediate access to the national park trail network. The Trilho dos Currais and the Pedra Bela viewpoint trail begin nearby. The Gerês valley wildflowers in spring, the wolf survey nights run by the park authority, and the traditional granite villages above the reservoir are the primary attractions.

## 3. Parque de Campismo do Gameiro, Figueiró dos Vinhos, Leiria

In the Zêzere valley of central Portugal near the Castelo de Bode reservoir, this municipal campsite has direct lake access and provides canoe and kayak hire. The Castelo de Bode reservoir is Portugal's largest artificial lake; the surrounding pine and eucalyptus forests of the Serra de Lousã are good cycling terrain. The camp is well-positioned for the Schist Villages — a network of slate-built mountain hamlets that have been restored as heritage tourism sites.

## 4. Parque Carlos Pinto, Arganil, Coimbra

In the Serra do Açor near the Mata da Margaraça — a relict ancient oak forest that survived the eucalyptus and pine monoculture conversion of most Portuguese hillside forests — this campsite has a forested setting and river access. The Margaraça forest walk gives a sense of the pre-colonial Portuguese upland vegetation.

## 5. Cepo Verde, Vila Pouca de Aguiar, Vila Real

In the northern wine country between the Douro Valley and the Montesinho Natural Park, Cepo Verde is a well-regarded private site in Trás-os-Montes — the most culturally distinct of Portugal's regions, with a linguistic and culinary identity separate from the mainstream. The Douro wine terraces are 40 km south.

## 6. Parque de Campismo de Monsanto, Castelo Branco

Near the remarkable village of Monsanto in the Beira Interior — a village of granite houses built between enormous boulders on a rocky hilltop, voted most Portuguese village in 1938 — this campsite is in the shadow of the medieval castle. The Beira Interior is the least visited region of Portugal's mainland interior, with a spare, arid landscape distinct from the Atlantic north.

## 7. Parque de Campismo do Fundão, Fundão, Castelo Branco

In the Serra da Gardunha foothills of the Beira Interior, the Fundão municipal camp is a base for the cherry blossom season (March-April) for which the valley is famous, and for summer cycling in the serra. The Serra da Estrela mountains — Portugal's highest at 1,993 metres — are 40 km northeast.

## 8. Parque de Campismo Picheleiros, Setúbal

In the Serra da Arrábida Natural Park — a limestone coastal range south of Lisbon with extraordinary cliff scenery and some of the cleanest seawater in Portugal — the Picheleiros site provides access to the Arrábida Marine Natural Reserve's protected beaches. The Arrabida limestone produces a geological landscape unlike anywhere else in Portugal; the clifftop walks give views from Setúbal to the Troia peninsula.

## 9. Parque de Campismo dos Fetáis, Peniche, Leiria

On the coast near Peniche — a fishing port and surfing destination on the Atlantic coast north of Lisbon — this campsite has access to Supertubos beach, one of the most powerful beach breaks in Europe and a regular stop on the World Surf League circuit. The Berlengas Islands, a UNESCO biosphere reserve 10 km offshore, are accessible by ferry from Peniche and have restricted camping by permit.

## 10. Markadia, Sintra, Lisbon

In the Sintra hills above Lisbon — a UNESCO World Heritage landscape of romantic palaces, quintas, and dense moss-covered forest — Markadia is the most convenient forest camping option within the Sintra-Cascais Natural Park. Sintra's palaces (Pena, Monserrate, Regaleira) and the Atlantic Cape Roca — the westernmost point of continental Europe — are within cycling distance.

## FCMP card and booking

The FCMP camping card provides discounts at affiliated sites nationally. Portuguese municipal campsites are often significantly cheaper than equivalent private sites; ACSI CampingCard coverage is more limited here than in France or Germany. The Algarve sites require booking for July and August; northern Portugal sites typically take arrivals without reservation outside peak weeks.
