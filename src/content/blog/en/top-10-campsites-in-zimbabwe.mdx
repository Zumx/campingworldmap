Zimbabwe is among Africa's great undiscovered camping destinations. The combination of Hwange National Park — one of the continent's largest elephant populations — with the Zambezi floodplain at Mana Pools, the ancient rock-art landscape of Matobo Hills, and the Victoria Falls at Livingstone-Kazungula makes for a circuit of extraordinary quality. Camping infrastructure, which declined during the economic difficulties of the 2000s, has recovered significantly and the Zimbabwe Parks and Wildlife Management Authority (ZimParks) now manages a range of sites from communal campgrounds to exclusive campsites allocated to single parties. The exclusive campsite model, pioneered in Zimbabwe and widely adopted elsewhere in Africa, provides genuine wilderness solitude.

## 1. Main Camp Campsite, Hwange National Park

The main ZimParks camp at Hwange — the park covering 14,650 square kilometres and holding the largest elephant population on earth (estimated at 45,000+ animals) — provides fenced camping with waterhole views. The Nyamandlovu Platform waterhole, adjacent to the camp, reliably produces elephant sightings throughout the day and illuminated views at night. Lion, leopard, sable antelope, and painted wolf (African wild dog) are all present in the park.

## 2. Sinamatella Campsite, Hwange National Park

In the northern sector of Hwange — the area with the highest density of predators in the park — Sinamatella camp is on a rocky escarpment with views across the Sinamatella valley. The camp has a waterhole below the escarpment that attracts large elephant herds, buffalo, and the resident predator groups. The remote location (150 km from the main gate on dirt roads) ensures fewer visitors than the southern camps.

## 3. Masuma Dam Campsite (Exclusive Camp), Hwange

One of Hwange's exclusive campsites — allocated to one party only at a premium price — Masuma Dam sits at the edge of the dam that is among the most productive wildlife-watching points in the park. The allocation means no other parties share the site; the privacy and the immediate waterhole access combine for a genuinely remarkable safari camping experience.

## 4. Ngweshla Camp (Exclusive Camp), Hwange

Another exclusive Hwange camp, Ngweshla is positioned at a large open pan in the central Hwange plains. The grass plains around Ngweshla attract large herds of zebra, wildebeest, and eland, which in turn attract the park's lion and painted wolf (wild dog) packs. Painted wolf packs in Hwange's central area number up to 30 individuals — the largest pack sizes in Africa.

## 5. Robins Camp Campsite, Hwange National Park

In the remote northwest of Hwange near the Botswana border, Robins Camp is the least visited of the main Hwange camps and has the feel of a park that existed before mass tourism. The camp is adjacent to woodland waterholes that produce big-cat activity at night. The drive to Robins Camp on Hwange's internal tracks can take five hours from the main gate — it is genuinely remote.

## 6. Jambili Campsite, Hwange

A public campsite in the eastern Hwange woodlands, Jambili is positioned in sable antelope and roan antelope habitat — two of Zimbabwe's most visually spectacular large antelopes, both relatively rare elsewhere in Africa. The Zimbabwe teak woodland here is the most intact of this ecosystem type on earth.

## 7. Mana Pools Camping, Zambezi National Park

The Mana Pools National Park on the Zambezi floodplain is one of Africa's most extraordinary camping experiences — a UNESCO World Heritage Site where the exclusive campsite system places single parties alone in the bush along the river. Hippos, crocodiles, elephant, lion, and leopard use the same floodplain; walking safaris with a licenced guide are the primary activity. The dry-season (May through October) beach camps are the specific draw — sandy river banks with wildlife at immediate proximity.

## 8. Naletale Ruins Campsite, Midlands

Near the Naletale Zimbabwe (stone enclosure ruins) in the Midlands province — one of the most elaborately decorated of Zimbabwe's ancient stone enclosure sites, with decorative chevron and herringbone stone coursework of great technical sophistication — this camping area is a base for archaeology-focused visitors. The Zimbabwe ruins tradition, of which Great Zimbabwe is the most famous, represents the peak of the Shona civilisation between the eleventh and fifteenth centuries.

## 9. Toghwana Dam, Hwange periphery

On a private conservancy at the eastern edge of Hwange, Toghwana Dam is representative of the community and private conservancy camping that now extends the effective wildlife corridor of the Hwange ecosystem. The KAZA (Kavango-Zambezi) Transfrontier Conservation Area, which incorporates Hwange and extends into Botswana and Zambia, is one of the largest conservation zones on earth.

## 10. Jolly Boys Camp, Livingstone, Victoria Falls

Just across the Zambia border from Victoria Falls Zimbabwe, Jolly Boys Camp in Livingstone is the standard base camp for the falls experience and the Zambezi activities — white water rafting, bungee jumping, and game drives in the Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park. The campsite has good facilities and is well-connected to the logistics of the Victoria Falls tourism area.

## ZimParks exclusive campsite booking

Exclusive campsites at Hwange and Mana Pools must be booked through ZimParks directly or through licensed Zimbabwean tour operators. Prices are in USD. Availability for peak season (June through October) opens several months in advance; early booking is essential. All exclusive campsites require a self-sufficient camping setup — kitchen, sleeping equipment, and all food must be brought in.
