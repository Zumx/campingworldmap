Nepal is the definitive destination for high-altitude camping on Earth. Eight of the world's fourteen peaks exceeding 8,000 metres are entirely or partly within its borders, and the trekking infrastructure built up over six decades of mountaineering and adventure travel has made its high-altitude environments accessible to a wider range of campers than almost any other Himalayan region. From the crowded but spectacular Everest corridor in Khumbu to the remote Tibetan plateau landscapes of Upper Mustang, the diversity of camping terrain is extraordinary.

Nepal's trekking system operates on a framework of teahouses — family-run guesthouses providing accommodation and meals — along the major routes, which has reduced the need for fully self-sufficient camping for most trekkers. However, truly remote routes in Dolpo, Kangchenjunga, and the Kanchanjunga Conservation Area still require full camping expeditions with tents, cook staff, and food carried by porters. Understanding which style suits your objectives matters for planning. The [campsite map](/map) provides a geographic overview of Nepal's main trekking and camping regions before you read the site-specific highlights below.

## 1. Everest Base Camp Trek, Khumbu, Sagarmatha Zone

The 130-kilometre round trip from Lukla to Everest Base Camp at 5,364 metres is the world's most famous trek. The route passes through Namche Bazaar, Tengboche monastery, and the high camps of Dingboche and Lobuche before reaching the base camp glacier terminal. Camping is possible at each stopping point alongside the teahouse options. The Khumbu Glacier and the surrounding peaks — Lhotse, Nuptse, Pumori — create a theatrical mountain setting unmatched anywhere. The Kala Patthar viewpoint at 5,545 metres provides the classic Everest view. The annual spring climbing season brings expedition activity to the base camp, while the October-November post-monsoon window is the preferred trekking season.

## 2. Annapurna Circuit Trek, Gandaki Zone

The Annapurna Circuit — historically a 21-day loop around the Annapurna massif — crosses the Thorong La pass at 5,416 metres and passes through an extraordinary diversity of terrain: subtropical forest, terraced farmland, arid canyon, high desert, and alpine grassland. The Mustang valley section in the north has a distinctly Tibetan character. Camping along the circuit is possible at designated sites, though the teahouse infrastructure is comprehensive along the main route. The Tilicho Lake detour to 4,919 metres is a highlight accessible from Manang; camping at the lake gives the most spectacular views. The southern Poon Hill route is a shorter, high-reward option for those with less time.

## 3. Langtang National Park Trek, Rasuwa District

The Langtang Valley trek north of Kathmandu is the most accessible high-altitude trek in Nepal — reachable from the capital by a four to five hour drive to the Syabrubesi trailhead. The valley is a glacially carved trough flanked by the Langtang and Ganesh Himal ranges, and the high point of Kyanjin Ri at 4,773 metres gives views of the Langtang massif. Camping is available throughout the valley, and Kyanjin Gompa has a small research station and basic campsite. The valley community was devastated by the 2015 earthquake, which triggered a landslide that buried Langtang village; the rebuilt community now welcomes trekkers as part of its recovery.

## 4. Manaslu Circuit Trek, Gorkha District

The Manaslu Circuit is considered Nepal's finest circuit trek for those who want the Annapurna Circuit atmosphere with considerably fewer fellow trekkers. The route circumnavigates the world's eighth-highest mountain and crosses the Larkya La pass at 5,213 metres. The Nubri and Tsum valleys in the northern sections have a strongly Tibetan Buddhist character — ancient gompas, prayer flags, and stone-built villages at high altitude. A restricted area permit is required for the northern sections. Camping along the route is well-established, and the Manaslu Conservation Area camping sites provide organized stops on the circuit.

## 5. Dolpo Circuit Trek, Dolpo District

Dolpo in northwestern Nepal is one of the most remote trekking regions in Asia — a high-altitude plateau at 4,000 to 5,000 metres with Tibetan cultural traditions largely unchanged by outside influence. Peter Matthiessen's book "The Snow Leopard" described an expedition through Dolpo that brought the region to international attention. The inner Dolpo restricted area (requiring a special permit) encompasses the Shey Gompa monastery and the Crystal Mountain sacred to Bon religion. Full camping expeditions with cook staff and porters are required. Snow leopard, Tibetan wolf, Himalayan blue sheep (bharal), and Tibetan gazelle inhabit the plateau.

## 6. Kangchenjunga Conservation Area Trek, Taplejung District

The Kangchenjunga Circuit in far eastern Nepal approaches the world's third-highest mountain from both its northern and southern base camps, crossing high passes and passing through pristine montane forest with rhododendron blooms of extraordinary scale in April and May. The region has a traditional Rai and Limbu cultural character in the lower valleys, transitioning to a Sherpa Buddhist atmosphere at higher elevations. Full camping is required throughout — no teahouse network exists on this route. Red pandas, clouded leopards, and snow leopards inhabit the forest zones. The combination of biodiversity, cultural richness, and remoteness makes Kangchenjunga Nepal's most complete wilderness trek.

## 7. Rara National Park Trek, Mugu District

Rara Lake in the remote Karnali zone of northwestern Nepal is the largest lake in Nepal, sitting at 2,990 metres in a forested basin surrounded by snow peaks. The national park protecting it is Nepal's smallest, but the combination of the lake's extraordinary beauty — a vivid blue-green color in an encircling mountain bowl — and the difficulty of access keeps it among the least visited parks in the Himalaya. Access is by small aircraft to Talcha airstrip followed by a two-day trek, or by a very long overland route from Jumla. Camping at the lakeside is the primary overnight option, and the forest around the lake holds musk deer, Himalayan black bear, and over 200 bird species.

## 8. Upper Mustang Trek, Mustang District

Upper Mustang — the former Kingdom of Lo — is a Tibetan plateau landscape that remained closed to foreigners until 1992. The restricted area permit system still limits visitor numbers, and the result is a pristine ancient Tibetan culture in a dramatically eroded canyon landscape of ochre, red, and white cliffs. The walled city of Lo Manthang, the kingdom's former capital, is the trek's destination, reached in five to seven days from Kagbeni through increasingly arid and spectacular terrain. Camping along the route uses established camping areas. The spring festival of Tiji at Lo Manthang is one of the most spectacular religious festivals in the Himalaya.

## 9. Gosaikund Trek, Rasuwa District

The sacred Hindu and Buddhist lake of Gosaikund at 4,380 metres is one of Nepal's most significant religious pilgrimage sites. During the August festival of Janai Purnima, tens of thousands of pilgrims walk to the lake from Sundarijal or Dhunche. Outside festival season, the trail is quiet and the alpine scenery — multiple sacred lakes set in a glacially carved bowl — is outstanding. Camping at the lakeside is the traditional pilgrim accommodation. The route connects to the Langtang valley over the Laurebina La pass, creating a rewarding crossing of the Langtang ridge. The lower Helambu valley section below the ridge provides a cultural immersion in Hyolmo Buddhist village life.

## 10. Phoksundo Lake, Shey Phoksundo National Park, Dolpo

Phoksundo Lake in the lower Dolpo region is Nepal's deepest lake and one of the most intensely colored in the Himalaya — a vivid turquoise-blue from copper minerals in the surrounding geology, contrasting against the arid brown and grey cliffs of the Dolpo plateau. The Suligad river gorge approach through dense forest transitions abruptly to the stark plateau landscape at the lake. Camping at the lakeshore is the primary overnight option, with basic teahouse accommodation at the adjacent village of Rigmo. The Phoksundo waterfall dropping into the canyon is one of the most photogenic natural features in all of Nepal. Dolpo is accessible by flight to Juphal airstrip followed by two to three days of trekking.
