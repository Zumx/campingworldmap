South Africa has one of the highest rates of camping participation in the world. The SANParks (South African National Parks) network of rest camps across Kruger, Garden Route, the Tankwa Karoo, and the Cape parks provides fenced, well-serviced camping with consistent standards. Private game reserves, community campsites on communal land, and the wilderness areas of the Cape Fold Mountains provide the full spectrum from resort-style camping to genuine backcountry. The country's excellent road infrastructure and the 4WD overland culture make it one of the most self-drive-friendly camping destinations in Africa.

## 1. Satara Rest Camp, Kruger National Park

In the central zone of Kruger — the area with the highest lion density in the park and productive grassland for large herds — Satara is the most wildlife-intensive of Kruger's main rest camps. The camp is fenced; walking within the camp is safe; driving the N'wanetsi and Sweni river loops from the camp produces big-cat sightings at reliably high rates in winter (dry season). Electric pitches, ablution blocks, a camp shop, and a restaurant.

## 2. Berg-en-Dal Rest Camp, Kruger National Park

In the hilly bushveld of southern Kruger near Malelane gate, Berg-en-Dal has a different vegetation character from the northern camps — more varied topography, denser vegetation, and good leopard country. White rhino populations in southern Kruger are recovering; Berg-en-Dal is one of the better camps for rhino sightings in the park. The dam at the camp's edge attracts hippo and bird life.

## 3. Beaverlac, Cederberg, Western Cape

In the Cederberg Mountains — a sandstone wilderness north of Cape Town with extraordinary rock formations, rock art, and fynbos — Beaverlac is a private farm campsite at a river gorge and natural swimming hole. The Cederberg's rock formations, including the Wolfberg Arch and the Maltese Cross sandstone formations, are within day-hiking range. The area holds San (Bushman) rock art sites estimated to be up to 8,000 years old. The campsite is basic; the landscape is exceptional.

## 4. Kliphuis, Cederberg

A CapeNature managed campsite deeper in the Cederberg wilderness than Beaverlac, Kliphuis is at the base of the sandstone peaks near Pakhuispas. The fynbos wildflowers in spring (August through October) here are excellent; the rock formations above the camp require scrambling to reach but offer dramatic views across the Cederberg plateau.

## 5. Tweede Tol, Cederberg

On the Driehoeks river in the northern Cederberg, Tweede Tol is a river gorge camping area accessed through a farm gate. The campsite is at a series of rock pools on the Driehoeks — swimming in summer, quiet and cold in winter. The surrounding mountains have good multi-day hiking with overnight hut options.

## 6. Orange Kloof Tented Camp, Table Mountain National Park

In the Southern suburbs section of Table Mountain National Park — the only piece of the park not directly accessible from Cape Town's suburbs — Orange Kloof requires a permit and guided entry. The overnight tented camp is the only way to experience the park interior above the Cape Peninsula. Leopard are resident in the Orange Kloof; dense Afrotemperate forest and the highest density of endemic fynbos make this the most biodiverse section of Cape Town's mountain.

## 7. Karoo National Park Camp, Beaufort West

In the Great Karoo — the vast semi-arid plateau that covers much of South Africa's interior — the Karoo National Park camp at Beaufort West is the principal Karoo camping destination. The park was established primarily to protect the Cape mountain zebra; black rhino, springbok, and red hartebeest are resident. The night sky in the Karoo is among the best in the world — the MeerKAT radio telescope array is nearby, the region was chosen for its extraordinary atmospheric clarity.

## 8. Salmonsdam Nature Reserve, Overberg, Western Cape

In the Salmonsdam Nature Reserve in the Kleinrivier Mountains north of Hermanus, this campsite is in one of the more intact fynbos mountain landscapes in the Western Cape. Hermanus is the premier whale-watching town in the world; the southern right whale calving season (June through November) peaks in September and October, with whales visible from the shore. The campsite is simple; the wildflower spring is excellent.

## 9. Steenkampshoek Camp Site, Eastern Cape

In the mountainous Eastern Cape near Graaff-Reinet — the town in the valley of desolation, a geological formation of dolerite columns rising above the Karoo plains — this remote campsite is for self-sufficient visitors comfortable with limited facilities. The Eastern Cape Karoo has a distinct character from the Western Karoo; the biodiversity of the succulent Karoo biome here is high.

## 10. Fort Fordyce Nature Reserve Camp Site, Eastern Cape

Near King William's Town (Bhisho) in the Eastern Cape, Fort Fordyce is a woodland nature reserve in the narrow-leafed ironwood and white stinkwood forest of the amaThole Mountains. Buffalo, leopard, and the amaThole endemic plants are the principal attractions. The reserve is less visited than the coastal or Karoo reserves and offers a genuine solitude experience.

## SANParks booking and fees

SANParks campsites must be booked through the SANParks.org system, which opens 11 months in advance. Kruger sites in July and August require booking at the maximum advance date. South African residents pay reduced Wild Card rates; international visitors pay full tariff. All SANParks camps operate a strict gate opening/closing system — arrivals after the posted closing time are refused.
